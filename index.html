The Opportunity

Behind every discovery, there is a supply chain.

When people hear "CERN," they think of the Higgs boson, of particle collisions at nearly the speed of light, of questions about the fundamental structure of the universe. What they rarely think about is the thousands of companies across Europe that make this work physically possible.

Every superconducting magnet, every vacuum chamber, every cable and sensor and cryogenic valve was designed, manufactured, and delivered by a firm — often a small or medium-sized enterprise — that learned to meet tolerances the rest of the market never asks for. These companies do not just supply parts. They become part of the research infrastructure itself.

And yet, many companies that could contribute to CERN's mission have never heard of CERN's procurement process. They do not know that opportunities exist, how to register, or what it means to be a supplier to big science.

The communication challenge is not about what CERN does. It is about who else belongs in the story.

This is a communication challenge. The science is already extraordinary. The engineering is already world-class. What is needed is a way to bridge the gap between what CERN needs and what industry can offer — and to do it through content that is clear, engaging, and built for the platforms where decision-makers actually spend their attention.

With the Future Circular Collider entering its preparatory phase, the next decade will bring an unprecedented wave of procurement opportunities — from civil engineering and cryogenics to detector components and sustainable materials. The moment to strengthen industry outreach is now.

Campaign Framework

"Your technology, our discoveries."

A proposed outreach campaign built on three communication pillars, each designed to move a different audience segment closer to engaging with CERN's procurement process.

01

Visibility

Many potential suppliers do not know CERN procures commercially. The first pillar focuses on awareness: making the procurement process visible to European industry through LinkedIn content, industry event presence, and ILO coordination. Content answers the question: "CERN buys from companies like ours?"

02

Accessibility

Companies that discover CERN procurement often find the entry process unclear. The second pillar simplifies the journey: clearer web content, visual guides to the tendering process, and a restructured procurement website that walks first-time visitors through registration step by step.

03

Community

Suppliers who have worked with CERN are the best advocates for future ones. The third pillar turns existing partners into storytellers: supplier spotlight features, video interviews, and case studies that show what working with CERN actually looks like from the industry side.

Social Media Campaign

LinkedIn content sequence.

Three posts designed for CERN's LinkedIn channel, sequenced across two weeks to build awareness of procurement opportunities among industry audiences.

Week 1 · Awareness

Behind every discovery, there is a supply chain.

When the Higgs boson was confirmed in 2012, the world celebrated a scientific milestone. Behind it were thousands of contracts with companies across CERN's Member States — firms that designed, manufactured, and delivered the components that made the discovery physically possible.

Today, CERN invests around 500 million Swiss Francs annually in goods and services from industry. From precision machining and cryogenic systems to civil engineering and IT infrastructure, the range of procurement needs is broad — and growing.

With the Future Circular Collider entering its preparatory phase, the next decade will bring unprecedented opportunities for European industry to contribute to fundamental research.

Week 2 · How-to

How to become a CERN supplier: 4 things to know.

1. Registration is free and open. Any company from a CERN Member or Associate Member State can register on the e-Procurement platform to receive notifications about relevant tendering opportunities.

2. Your national ILO is your first contact. Each Member State has an Industrial Liaison Officer who acts as a bridge between local industry and CERN's procurement team.

3. Size is not a barrier. CERN works with companies of all sizes. Many of its most critical suppliers are SMEs with specialised technical capabilities.

4. The FCC will expand demand significantly. The proposed 90.7 km Future Circular Collider is expected to generate substantial new procurement needs across multiple sectors.

Design rationale

The current procurement website leads with institutional information. This concept restructures the homepage around three visitor intents: companies exploring CERN for the first time ("How to work with us"), returning suppliers checking opportunities ("Open tenders"), and stakeholders seeking impact stories ("Supplier stories"). The hero banner foregrounds the campaign tagline, and the sidebar surfaces live opportunities without requiring extra clicks. Content is designed for WordPress block editor compatibility.
